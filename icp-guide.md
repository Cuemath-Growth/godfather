# CUEMATH ICP & TARGET AUDIENCE GUIDE

## Primary ICP Profile
- **Core:** Education-focused mother, 35–44, often South Asian/Indian-American
- **Psyche:** Researches before buying, distrusts gimmicks, requires multiple exposures
- **Values:** Education as long-term investment, conceptual understanding over grades

## Two Distinct ICPs

### ICP A: NRI Parent (70% of leads)
- Education-forward Indian diaspora mother
- 19.4% enrollment rate (6x higher than Non-NRI)
- 41.6% acquired through referrals (94% of referral leads are NRI)
- Child: 54.3% "average or at par," 42.3% "very good" — enrichment audience
- **Goals:** Fun/enjoyable math (49.1%), fundamentals/confidence (47.3%), maintain performance (40.6%)
- **Content Strategy:** Reinforce decision, lead with NRI social proof, vernacular testimonials work

### ICP B: Non-NRI Parent (30% of leads)
- US-based parent (any ethnicity), found via search/content
- 3.2% enrollment rate
- 74% of organic content leads are Non-NRI but only 0.1% convert
- **Goals:** Fun/enjoyable (53.7%), maintain performance (49.5%), fundamentals (44.6%)
- **Content Strategy:** Build case from scratch. Recognition → philosophy → trust sequence

## Four Behavioral Segments

### 1. Foundation Rebuilder (PRIMARY — 28.6%)
- **Trigger:** Child passes tests through memorization
- **Angle:** "Your child will understand the WHY behind math"

### 2. Personalization Seeker (CO-PRIMARY — 12.0%)
- **Trigger:** Child doesn't fit one-size-fits-all pace
- **Angle:** "A learning system built around your child"

### 3. Confidence Builder (SUPPORTING — 18.2%)
- **Trigger:** Child hesitant, doesn't volunteer
- **Angle:** "Watch your child become confident in math"

### 4. Accelerator (TERTIARY — 2.3%)
- **Trigger:** Exam pressure, advanced aspirations
- **Rule:** Serve but do NOT target — reframe toward thinking depth

## Content Mix Rule
For every 10 pieces: 4 Foundation Rebuilder, 3 Personalization Seeker, 2 Confidence Builder, 1 universal/brand

## What They're Actually Buying (Ranked)
1. Understanding (49.9%): Child grasps the "why"
2. Confidence (46.8%): Child stops dreading, starts volunteering
3. Personalized system (42.1%): Tutor + curriculum + platform together

## CRITICAL: Enrichment, Not Remediation
- Only 2.2% describe child as "struggling"
- Content: "your child could understand math more deeply" NOT "is your child falling behind?"
- Only 2% use dramatic anxiety language — most describe softer gaps

## Content Formula
Lead with emotion → Anchor to system → Close on outcome

## The Tutor + System Dynamic
- 60.8% mention tutor by name/role (emotional anchor)
- 34.5% credit Cuemath brand
- **Position:** Lead with emotional connection (tutor), build brand around system

## Decision-Making
- Mother is lead researcher and decision-maker
- 26.6% use "we" language (household decision)
- Speak to her, don't exclude him

## Child Profile
- Grades 1–8 (ages 6–14), peak 7–12
- Nearly equal sons/daughters — represent both
- Emotional state: could be more confident, gets by but doesn't truly understand

## Anti-ICP: The Shortcut Seeker
- Wants quick results, guaranteed grades, price sensitivity
- **Repelled by:** Understanding-first messaging, investment framing, long-term confidence
- Content pre-qualifies and protects retention

## Channel Performance (NRI)
- Referrals: 41.6% conversion
- Organic brand: 34.0%
- Google brand paid: 28.4%
- Meta ads: 15.1%

## Channel Performance (Non-NRI)
- Referrals: 37.3% (tiny volume)
- Meta ads: 0.6% — essentially non-converting
- Organic non-brand: 0.1%

## Pain Points (from negative reviews)
- 42.8%: Billing/pricing friction
- 42.8%: Post-payment silence
- 40.4%: Tutor quality inconsistency
- 33.2%: App/platform issues

## Voice Rules
- Calm, intelligent, empathetic — never flashy or salesy
- Use parent's actual language: "understands the why," "patient teacher," "enjoying math now"
- Never use jargon like "neuro-adaptive learning pathway"
