## BRAND ESSENCE

- **Brand:** Cuemath
- **Promise:** A personalized math ecosystem building deep understanding, confidence, and real-world problem-solving — for school, competitions, and life.
- **Tagline:** "Making Kids MathFit(TM)"
- **Master Frame:** "AI calculates. Humans must think."
- **North Star:** If someone sees ONLY one Cuemath ad in their life, they should walk away thinking: "This company isn't just teaching math. They're preparing kids to think in the AI world."

## MATHFIT(TM) FRAMEWORK

MathFit = mathematical fitness. Two dimensions:

### Skills (FUAR — ALWAYS this order, never reorder)
1. **Fluency** — accurate, efficient execution of operations
2. **Understanding** — deep conceptual clarity, the "why" behind the "what"
3. **Application** — solving word problems and real-world math
4. **Reasoning** — logical thinking, pattern recognition, strategy

### Mindset
- Interest, Confidence, Growth Mindset & Resilience, Math Relevance

### MathFit IS / IS NOT
| IS | IS NOT |
|---|---|
| Thinking | Speed tricks |
| Understanding | Finishing worksheets |
| Confidence | Memorising formulas |
| Reasoning through unfamiliar problems | Racing to answers |

### Rules
- TM on first mention. Can omit in body copy after first prominent use. Must reappear in headers, end frames, sign-offs.
- Correct: MathFit(TM). Incorrect: MathFit / mathfit / Math Fit
- The App (MathGym) uses 5 skills including Memory. Marketing ALWAYS uses FUAR (4 skills only).

## BRAND PERSONALITY: THE GURU-COACH

Not a Western life coach. The Guru illuminates the path without walking it for you.

**Be:** Wise, Empowering, Trusted, Human
**Never be:** Cheerleader, Pressure machine, Cold/clinical, Arrogant

## VOICE & TONE

**Voice (constant):** Authoritative without condescending. Direct and precise. Energising. Honest. Curious.
**Tone (varies):** Acquisition = confident, aspirational. Lead with what the child becomes. Never open with fear or deficit.

Who we sound like: Confident, warm, precise. A trusted advisor who knows math education deeply.
Who we don't sound like: Excited startup. Children's brand with cartoon energy. Generic edtech.

Tone spectrum:
- Confident (not salesy)
- Warm (not fluffy)
- Precise (not jargon-heavy)

## AUDIENCE

### Primary ICP
Education-focused mother, 35-44, often South Asian/Indian-American. Researches before buying. Distrusts gimmicks. Requires multiple exposures.

### ICP A: NRI Parent (70% of leads)
- 19.4% enrollment rate (6x higher than Non-NRI)
- 41.6% via referrals (94% of referral leads are NRI)
- Content: Reinforce decision, lead with NRI social proof, vernacular testimonials work
- Tone: Warmer, community-oriented

### ICP B: Non-NRI Parent (30% of leads)
- 3.2% enrollment rate
- Content: Build case from scratch. Recognition -> philosophy -> trust sequence
- Tone: Proof/philosophy-oriented

### Four Behavioural Segments
1. **Foundation Rebuilder (28.6%):** "Your child will understand the WHY behind math"
2. **Personalization Seeker (12.0%):** "A learning system built around your child"
3. **Confidence Builder (18.2%):** "Watch your child become confident in math"
4. **Accelerator (2.3%):** Serve but do NOT target. Reframe toward thinking. HIGHEST CHURN RISK.

Content mix per 10 pieces: 4 Foundation, 3 Personalization, 2 Confidence, 1 universal.

### What They're Actually Buying (ranked)
1. Understanding (49.9%): Child grasps the "why"
2. Confidence (46.8%): Child stops dreading, starts volunteering
3. Personalized system (42.1%): Tutor + curriculum + platform together

### CRITICAL: Enrichment, Not Remediation
Only 2.2% describe child as "struggling." Frame: "could understand more deeply" NOT "falling behind."

### Anti-ICP: The Shortcut Seeker
Wants quick results, guaranteed grades, price sensitivity. Repelled by understanding-first messaging. Content pre-qualifies and protects retention.

## POSITIONING

### Zone We Own
Excellence Through Productive Struggle — white space, nobody else is here.

### vs Competitors
- Kumon/Mathnasium: They remediate. We build thinkers.
- Speed math: They teach tricks. We teach reasoning.
- Russian School: They pressure. We coach.

### The Core Frames
- "AI calculates. Humans must think." (Calculating = mechanical, procedural, fragile. Thinking = reasoning, flexible, durable.)
- "Math makes sense when taught the right way." (Never: "Math is easy.")
- Transformation: Anxiety -> Confidence. Passive calculation -> Active reasoning. Fragile memorization -> Deep understanding.

4. **NEVER LEAD:** AI, technology, worksheet volume, speed, grade guarantees

AVOID: Statistics alone, app-store ratings, AI features, offer-heavy content

## THE CUEMATH WAY — PEDAGOGY

- **"Cue, don't tell"** — Tutors ask questions, children discover answers. Builds thinking, not dependency.
- **Productive Struggle** — Problems just hard enough to stretch thinking. Struggle is the path, not a bug.
- **Talk-o-Meter** — Child does the talking. "The more they talk, the better they think."
- **Interleaved Learning** — Mixes problem types for flexible pattern recognition.

## BANNED WORDS — NEVER USE

classroom, center, centre, unlock potential, bright future, love for learning, amazing, incredible, powerful, capable strong and confident, kiddish, guaranteed marks, guaranteed grades, quick results, fast improvement, easy math, math is easy, speed tricks, shortcuts

## REQUIRED SUBSTITUTIONS

- "capable, strong and confident" -> "think clearly, reason deeply, solve confidently"
- "classroom learning" -> "online learning" / "1:1 sessions"
- "our centre/center" -> "our program" / "Cuemath's platform"
- Generic confidence -> Specific outcome: "reason through problems, not just memorise steps"
- "speed and accuracy" -> "accuracy and fluency" (MathFit is not about speed)

## COPY PRINCIPLES

1. Outcomes over feelings. What the child will DO, not how they will FEEL.
2. Parent-facing always. Never address children directly.
3. Specific over abstract. Name the observable outcome.
4. No duplicate words within a copy block (morphological variants count).
5. Anchor in the framework — tie back to a specific MathFit dimension.
