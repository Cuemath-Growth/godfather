## 1. SMS Nurture Sequence (QL → Trial Booking)

Reviewed 4 SMS messages for the QL-to-Trial-Booking nurture flow. Key findings:

**What was wrong:**
- SMS1 ("you were so close!") — generic cart-abandonment language, no value reminder
- SMS2 & SMS3 — over-indexed on stats (400K families, 4.9 stars) which brand guidelines deprioritize vs. transformation stories
- Tone across all 4 was functional but flat — read like generic edtech, not Cuemath's MathFit™ voice

**What was strong:**
- SMS4 (process explainer) was the best — concrete, personalized, tells the parent what actually happens in the free class

**Recommended sequence order:**
1. Abandonment nudge — remind *why* it's worth finishing (tutor + thinking focus)
2. What they'll get — reduce uncertainty about the free class (strongest SMS)
3. Tutor matching — emotional, personalization angle
4. Challenge assumption — re-engage cold leads with "understand vs. do" framing

**Rewrites provided** for all 4 messages aligned to brand guidelines.

## 2. Brand Awareness Campaign — Strategic Decisions

### The Core Question
Should Cuemath be the hero, or should the parent who chooses Cuemath be the hero?

### The Debate
Explored three angles:

| Approach | Strength | Weakness |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Cuemath as hero | Category definition, brand recall | Self-congratulatory, ICP distrusts brand-forward |
| Parent as hero | Matches referral behavior (41.6% conversion), emotional | "Empowered parent" is crowded D2C territory |
| Belief as hero | Creates a cultural position only Cuemath can own | Risk: beliefs don't book trials if brand is unknown |

### Counter-arguments tested:
- "Belief campaigns are a luxury of market leaders" — **rebutted** by the fact that Cuemath has 12 years in market, 200K students, and high NRI community awareness already
- "Thinking vs. calculating is a strategist's frame, not a mother's frame" — valid concern, addressed by grounding every execution in real parent language and child moments, not abstract philosophy
- "Brand awareness is hard to attribute" — addressed with specific flywheel connecting awareness audiences to existing lead gen campaigns

### Final strategic decision:
**The belief is the hero.** Neither brand nor parent alone — but a worldview they share: *"Cuemath doesn't teach math. They teach kids how to think."*

This works because:
- Cuemath already has awareness in the NRI community — it needs *re-categorization*, not introduction
- The MathFit™ concept is a genuine differentiator backed by pedagogy, not just marketing language
- The NRI WhatsApp ecosystem amplifies belief-led content organically

## 3. Campaign Architecture

### The One Belief (Everything Ladders Here)
> "Cuemath doesn't teach math. They teach kids how to think."

### Format
- **Channels:** Meta (Instagram/Facebook) + YouTube
- **Duration:** 12 months
- **Monthly output:** 7–8 pieces
- **Annual output:** ~90 pieces
- **Not a TVC campaign** — a sustained content universe with recurring series that build familiarity over time

### 6 Series + Quarterly Anchors

| # | Series | Freq | Length | Role |
|---|--------|------|--------|------|
| 1 | **MathFit™ vs. Math Done** | 2x/mo | 30–45s | Category definer — split-screen contrast between "getting answers" and "understanding why" |
| 2 | **The Real Story** | 1x/mo | 60–90s | Docu-style parent transformation stories. 4-act structure: "everyone told me → but I noticed → so I chose → and now" |
| 3 | **AI Can Do Math** | 1x/mo | 15–30s | Zeitgeist hook. Provocative, shareable. AI vs. human thinking contrast. Designed for reach + WhatsApp forwarding |
| 4 | **The WhatsApp Clip** | 2x/mo | 15–20s | UGC-aesthetic. Looks like something a parent shot and shared. Three types: kid moments, screen recordings, parent real-talk |
| 5 | **The Tutor Moment** | Alt. months | 45–60s | Product differentiation. Shows how Cuemath tutors teach differently — "cue don't tell" in action |
| 6 | **In Their Own Words** | Alt. months | 30–60s | Real kids explaining math concepts to camera. The product demo disguised as aspiration |
| A | **The Cuemath Standard** | Quarterly | 90–120s | Premium anchor pieces. Q1: tutor selection. Q2: parent roundtable. Q3: MathFit™ manifesto. Q4: year in review |

### Content detail
- **Series 1** has 24 specific episode scenarios mapped for the full year across 4 batches (Foundation Concepts → Everyday Thinking → The Stakes → The Future)
- **Series 2** has market-specific breakdowns: 6 US NRI stories, 3 Australia NRI (NAPLAN, selective school, year-level), 3 UK/MEA
- **Series 3** has 12 specific AI-vs-thinking executions scripted
- **Series 4** has 24 clip concepts across 3 types (kid moments, screen moments, parent real-talk)
- **Series 5 & 6** have 6 executions each, fully described

All details in the full deck.

## 4. Channel Strategy Summary

### Meta
- **4 campaign tiers:** Reach (35%), Engagement (35%), Depth (20%), Community (10%)
- **NRI targeting:** South Asian diaspora interests + parenting + geo-fenced to top NRI metros
- **Retargeting flywheel:** 50%+ video viewers from awareness → fed into existing lead gen campaigns as warm audience
- **Key bet:** CPQL for brand-engaged audiences should be measurably lower than cold NRI targeting. This is the proof point.

### YouTube
- **Bumper ads (6s):** Series 3 distilled — frequency and recall. See 4–6x before encountering longer content
- **TrueView in-stream (skippable):** Series 1, 2, 5, 6, A — hook in 5 seconds, tell the story
- **Shorts:** Series 1, 3, 4 — same cuts as Meta Reels, native to Shorts feed
- **Discovery (in-feed):** Series 2, A — intent-driven, thumbnail + title drives clicks
- **Sequential ad serving:** Bumper → Short format → Long format → Lead gen. Audiences move through a funnel over time.

### Cross-Channel Flywheel
```
YT Bumpers (curiosity) → Meta Reels (understanding) → Meta Feed / YT TrueView (conviction) → Lead gen retarget (conversion)
```

## 7. Non-Negotiables (Alignment Checklist)

- [ ] Every piece ladders to: "Cuemath teaches thinking, not just math"
- [ ] MathFit™ positioned as a category, not a product feature
- [ ] Enrichment framing always — never imply the child is struggling
- [ ] Mother is the primary audience — speak to her intelligence
- [ ] Authenticity over production value (especially Series 2 and 4)
- [ ] Brand reveals late (last 3–5 seconds in most series)
- [ ] Content should repel shortcut seekers — that's a feature
- [ ] Every exposed audience flows into lead gen — track the handoff
