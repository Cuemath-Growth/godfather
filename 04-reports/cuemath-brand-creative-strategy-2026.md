## 2. Who We're Talking To

### The ICP in One Line

A thoughtful, education-forward parent — most often a mother aged 35–44 — who believes deep mathematical thinking and confidence under pressure are the foundation of long-term success. This is a belief-based ICP, not a demographic slice. It holds across all geographies.

### Two Distinct Audiences

**NRI Parent (primary):** Education-forward Indian diaspora mother. Acquired largely through referrals and community trust. Goals: fun/enjoyable math, fundamentals/confidence, maintain performance. Strategy: reinforce decision, lead with NRI social proof, vernacular testimonials work.

**Non-NRI Parent (secondary):** US-based parent (any ethnicity), found via search/content. Goals: fun/enjoyable, maintain performance, fundamentals. Strategy: build case from scratch — Recognition → Philosophy → Trust sequence.

### What Parents Are Actually Buying

1. **Understanding (49.9%)** — child grasps the "why"
2. **Confidence (46.8%)** — child stops dreading, starts volunteering
3. **Personalized system (42.1%)** — tutor + curriculum + platform together

Only 2.2% describe their child as "struggling." This is an enrichment buyer, not a remediation buyer.

### Parent Personas

| Persona | Mindset | What They Seek |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Goal-Oriented Parent** | Values academic excellence | Structured improvement, measurable progress |
| **Anxious Parent** | Worried about learning gaps | Reassurance, steady visible progress, safety |
| **Exploration-Seeking Parent** | Wants future-ready education | Well-rounded thinking, beyond grades |

### Performance Marketing Segments

| Segment | Content Weight |
|---------|---------------|
| **Foundation Rebuilder** — "Your child will understand the WHY behind math" | 4 in 10 pieces |
| **Personalization Seeker** — "A learning system built around your child" | 3 in 10 pieces |
| **Confidence Builder** — "Watch your child become confident in math" | 2 in 10 pieces |
| **Accelerator** — Exam pressure, advanced aspirations | 1 in 10 — serve, don't target |

### The Anti-ICP: The Shortcut Seeker

Wants quick results, guaranteed grades, price sensitivity. **Repelled by:** understanding-first messaging, investment framing, long-term confidence. Our content should pre-qualify and protect retention.

### Decision-Making

Mother is the lead researcher and decision-maker. 26.6% use "we" language (household decision). Speak to her, don't exclude him.

## 3. Brand & Positioning

### The Core Problem

Cuemath has a **categorization problem**, not an awareness problem. NRI parents already know Cuemath — but they bucket it alongside Kumon, Mathnasium, and local tutors under generic "math tutoring." The job is to move Cuemath off that list entirely.

### The MathFit™ Framework (FUAR)

| Letter | Pillar | What It Means |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| **F** | Fluency | Speed and accuracy with number operations |
| **U** | Understanding | The "why" behind the method |
| **A** | Application | Using math to solve real-world and novel problems |
| **R** | Reasoning | Logical thinking under uncertainty |

**Mindset Dimension:** Interest · Confidence · Growth Mindset & Resilience · Math Relevance

MathFit™ is the proprietary category Cuemath is building — not a product feature, not a tagline. A state of mathematical readiness that only Cuemath occupies.

### The Cuemath Way — How We Teach

- **"Cue, Don't Tell"** — Tutors ask questions so the child discovers answers. Builds thinking, not dependency.
- **Productive Struggle** — Problems just hard enough to stretch thinking. Struggle is the path.
- **High Talk Ratio (Talk-o-Meter)** — The child does the talking and explaining. Cuemath measures it.
- **Interleaved Learning** — Mixes problem types to build flexible pattern recognition.

All neuroscience-backed: productive struggle, interleaving, retrieval practice, explanation, teaching back.

### The 5 USPs

| # | USP | What It Means for the Parent |
|---|-----|------------------------------|
| 1 | **Handpicked Tutors** | Top 1% certified, trained in math & child psychology. "Coaches who specialize in *your* child's thinking." |
| 2 | **One Personalized Program** | From school success to future readiness — all in one plan. |
| 3 | **The Cuemath Way** | Deep conceptual understanding that stays for life. |
| 4 | **Custom-Built Platform** | Collaborative smart whiteboard, gamified engagement. |
| 5 | **Proven Outcomes** | 4.9 Trustpilot, 200K students. "Confidence first, grades follow." |

### Competitive Positioning

| Dimension | Cuemath | Kumon | Mathnasium | Private Tutors | Online Platforms |

| **Method** | "Cue Don't Tell" — guided discovery | Worksheet repetition | Center-based instruction | Homework help | Self-paced video/app |

## 4. Messaging & Voice

### Messaging Hierarchy

1. **Lead with:** Tutor + system together; child's emotional transformation
2. **Support with:** Understanding and confidence as MathFit™ outcomes
3. **Reinforce:** Personalization and school alignment
4. **Never lead with:** AI, technology, worksheet volume, speed, or grade guarantees

### Brand Voice

The Mentor — wise, patient, clear, confident without pressure. Simple, warm, intelligent. Like a smart friend who happens to be an expert.

### Transformation Narrative (Before → After)

- Math anxiety → Math confidence
- Passive calculation → Active reasoning
- Fragile memorization → Deep understanding
- School struggle → Confident problem-solving
- Average performance → Outlier thinking

### Copy Atoms

- "MathFit™ isn't about getting answers faster. It's about thinking better."
- "Tricks fade. Understanding compounds."
- "The future belongs to thinkers, not human calculators."
- "We make it meaningful, not easy."
- "Genius isn't born. It's built—one good struggle at a time."
- "Real math confidence comes from figuring things out yourself."
- "If your child can explain why, they'll never forget how."
- "We're not prepping for the next test. We're prepping for the next world."

### DO's and DON'Ts

**DO:**
- Sound calm, confident, intelligent
- Speak to parents as thinking adults
- Make MathFit™ feel obvious and aspirational
- Show thinking transformations (not vanity wins)
- Use the "thinking vs. calculating" contrast frequently
- Frame tutors as "coaches who specialize in your child's thinking"
- Anchor messaging in outcomes, testimonials, retention data

**DON'T:**
- Promise shortcuts or hype speed/tricks
- Say "guaranteed marks" or "math is easy"
- Over-index on ease or fun
- Use childish visuals, loud colors, gimmicky stock imagery
- Compete in "math learning" as a commodity category
- Sound remedial or transactional

**Say:** "Math makes sense when taught the right way"
**Never say:** "Math is easy"

### Proof Formats (Ranked)

1. Parent transformation stories
2. Named tutor-within-system testimonials
3. Multi-year/sibling stories
4. Child's voice testimonials
5. MathFit philosophy content
- **Avoid:** Statistics alone, app-store ratings, AI features, offer-heavy content

### Trademark Usage

Always MathFit™ (with ™) on first mention, headlines, hero sections, ads. After first mention: ™ may be omitted in body copy for readability. Must reappear in section headers, end frames, logos.

**Universal structure:** HOOK → PROBLEM → SOLUTION → PROOF → CTA

## 10. Non-Indian Asian Expansion — Creative & Messaging Strategy

*Source: 32 in-depth interviews with US Non-Indian Asian parents (Chinese, Korean, Vietnamese, Filipino).*

### The Strategic Wedge: "Kumon Rehab"

The market is polarized between "Drill and Kill" (Kumon) and "Elite Competition" (AoPS). Our primary acquisition attack vector: **target the massive volume of parents experiencing "Kumon fatigue."**

> "Keep the discipline, ditch the drudgery."

We offer a "Balanced Pragmatist" solution: keep the intense cognitive discipline and routine they demand, but replace rote repetition with deep conceptual thinking and human-led engagement.

### Cultural Insight: "Chi Ku" (Eating Bitterness)

Parents value productive struggle and discipline. They distrust programs that are "too easy" or "just playing games." They want rigor without the soul-crushing trauma of legacy programs. **Cuemath's pedagogy is a natural fit** — productive struggle is literally our method.

### The "Seriousness Filter"

Before reading details, these parents ask: *"Is this a serious math track?"* Signals they require:

- **Tutor credentials** — generic tutors are rejected instantly. They demand credentialed experts (PhDs, former professors). This is the "Specific Teacher Cult."
- **Structured progression** — visible pathway map, not vague promises
- **Progress measurement** — the diagnostic must continue post-payment with scheduled reassessments
- **The "WeChat Brag Test"** — progress must map to Relative Superiority (Grade Level +2, percentile, competition readiness) so parents can socially defend the choice

### Discovery Sequence

**Brand awareness is zero** in this segment (32/32 had not heard of Cuemath).

1. **Private Seeding (The "Coupon Loop"):** Discovery happens in closed WeChat groups where "Alpha Parents" share monetized referral codes.
2. **Public Validation (The "Seriousness Filter"):** Parents immediately vet seeded names on public forums (Wenxuecity, Reddit) for comparative reasoning and "proof of rigor."

**Operational ask:** Build a double-sided WeChat-compatible referral program and publish **un-gated, highly granular curriculum maps (PDFs)**. If our curriculum is a "black box," it cannot be peer-reviewed by the community, and we will not be shortlisted.

### Messaging by Segment

| Segment | The Play |
|---------|----------|
| **Competitive/Advanced** | "Seriousness Umbrella" — prove elite reasoning depth vs AoPS benchmark |
| **Catch-up/Insurance** | "Kumon Rehab" — discipline without drudgery, rescue from Kumon fatigue |
| **Learning Centers** | Center-level seriousness, online — replicate assessment→plan→accountability |
| **App Graduates** | Beyond DIY apps — human-led engagement vs gamified streaks |
| **Private Tutor** | Validation-first — match tutor quality + continuity guarantee |
| **HS Confidence** | Validation-first — teen's judgment is decisive |

### Wave 0 Requirements (Pre-Launch)

Ship un-gated curriculum PDFs, "Master Class" tutor profiles, explicit placement assessments, "Exit Safety" policies, teacher-comment dashboards. **We cannot launch Wave 1 until the product matches the cultural expectations.**

## 12. Non-Negotiables

1. **Enrichment, never remediation.** Only 2.2% of parents describe their child as struggling.
2. **MathFit™ is a category, not a feature.**
3. **The belief is the hero** — thinking > calculating.
4. **Mothers are the primary audience.** Speak to her intelligence.
5. **Brand reveals late.** Last 3–5 seconds in most content.
6. **Content should repel shortcut seekers.** That's a feature.
7. **No urgency tactics.** Parents who take longer to trial convert better.
8. **The creative is always the unit of analysis.**
