# Cuemath Leads Intelligence Report — Q1 2026
## Deep Demographic Analysis & Growth Playbook
### Source: CRM Leads + PLA Data — 8,769 QLs (Jan 1 – Mar 31, 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

Analysis of 8,769 qualified leads from Q1 2026 reveals that **Q1 alone represents 89% of the entire historical dataset** (9,830 QLs across Jan 2024–Apr 2026) — confirming that Cuemath's paid acquisition machine scaled dramatically in this quarter. The headline findings from the full-period report hold: mothers convert 35% better than fathers end-to-end, NRI leads convert at 2.5x Non-NRI, and MCNA assessment remains a 1.5x conversion multiplier.

However, Q1 surfaces three critical new signals:
1. **NRI share is declining** — from 77.7% in January to 59.5% in March. This is the primary driver of falling conversion rates.
2. **Pricing objections have shifted to NRI** — 79.4% of pricing objections now come from your core segment, up from 44.8% in the full-period report. Value perception is eroding.
3. **Competitive losses are accelerating** — 113 leads lost to competitors in one quarter, nearly matching the 118 total across the full 2+ year dataset.

**Key metrics:**
- **QLs:** 8,769
- **Trial Done:** 2,517 (28.7% QL→TD)
- **Paid:** 753 (8.6% QL→Paid)
- **NRI QL→Paid:** 10.4% (6,192 leads → 645 paid)
- **Non-NRI QL→Paid:** 4.2% (2,577 leads → 108 paid)

**Key corrections applied during analysis:**
- PLA data (441 additional Meta leads) appended with deduplication by prospectid
- March 2026 cohort has only ~10 days of maturity — all March conversion rates will increase over time
- Gender classification based on name dictionary; 81.5% of leads remain unclassified
- All copy recommendations validated against MathFit™ brand guidelines

## SECTION 2: THE TRIAL VELOCITY PARADOX

### Finding: Q1 data DOES NOT confirm the full-period pattern

| Days from Lead to Trial | TD → Paid Rate | Count |
|------------------------|----------------|-------|
| 0-7 days | 29.4% | 1,729 |
| 8-14 days | 29.7% | 516 |
| 15-30 days | 25.1% | 183 |
| **30+ days** | **26.7%** | 60 |

**Full period showed:** 30+ day trialers at 34.7% TD→Paid, highest of all buckets.
**Q1 shows:** 30+ day trialers at 26.7% — actually *below* the 0-7 day bucket (29.4%).

**Median time to trial:** 5 days (unchanged from full period)

**Why the reversal?** Two likely explanations:
1. **Cohort maturity:** Q1 30+ day trialers are still within 0-40 days of their payment window. Some will convert in April-May, pulling this number up.
2. **Volume effect:** Only 60 leads in the 30+ day bucket (vs 1,729 in 0-7 days). Small sample sizes are noisy.

### Recommended Actions
- **Do NOT change the nurture extension strategy yet.** The full-period finding (34.7% for 30+ day trialers) is based on mature cohorts. Q1's 26.7% will likely climb as March/February leads mature.
- **Re-run this analysis in mid-May** when all Q1 leads have had 45+ days to convert. If 30+ day trialers still underperform, reconsider the nurture extension.
- **Continue extending nurture sequences to 45+ days** — the cost of nurture emails is near zero, and the worst case is you learn the pattern changed.
- **Segment nurture content by stage:**
  - Day 1-7: Social proof (other parents' experiences)
  - Day 8-21: Philosophy ("We teach children to think, not just calculate")
  - Day 22-45: Deep transformation stories

**Estimated impact:** Pending maturity re-check. Projected +30 paid per quarter if full-period pattern holds.

- **Do NOT lead with price comparisons** — this is transactional framing and violates brand positioning
- **Consider a "value realization" touchpoint** before the pricing conversation — show the assessment results, the personalized learning plan, and the tutor match before discussing cost.

### NRI Share by Month — THE EXPLANATORY VARIABLE

| Month | NRI QLs | NRI % | NRI QL→Paid | Non-NRI QLs | Non-NRI QL→Paid |
|-------|---------|-------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Jan 2026 | 2,251 | **77.7%** | 11.2% | 646 | 7.6% |
| Feb 2026 | 2,532 | **72.3%** | 9.7% | 971 | 3.1% |
| Mar 2026 | 1,409 | **59.5%** | 10.6% | 960 | 3.1% |

**What's real vs. artifact:**
- **Jan → Feb decline (10.4% → 7.9%):** This is real and significant. NRI share dropped from 77.7% → 72.3% AND NRI conversion itself dropped from 11.2% → 9.7%. Double hit: less NRI + worse NRI conversion.
- **Feb → Mar (7.9% → 7.5%):** Cannot be fully confirmed. March leads have only ~10 days of maturity. However, **NRI share collapsed to 59.5%** — an 18pp drop from January. Even if NRI conversion holds, the mix shift alone accounts for ~1.5pp of conversion decline.
- **NRI conversion within March (10.6%)** is actually *higher* than February (9.7%), suggesting NRI lead quality stabilized but volume dropped sharply. You're getting fewer NRI leads but better ones.
- **Non-NRI conversion stabilized at 3.1%** in both Feb and Mar — confirming Non-NRI has a structural floor around 3%.

### What Caused the NRI Share Drop?
Investigate these in order:
1. **Did Meta audience targeting broaden beyond core NRI segments in February/March?** Check lookalike percentages and interest targeting changes.
2. **Did PLA campaigns (which started Dec 2025) shift the audience mix?** PLA may attract a broader audience than traditional LP campaigns.
3. **Did budget shift between markets?** If US spend increased while APAC/ME held flat, the US audience (which has the highest non-NRI rate) would dilute the overall NRI share.
4. **Creative fatigue on NRI-targeted campaigns?** Same ads running for 60+ days may be exhausting the NRI audience on Meta.
