## Executive Summary

Analysis of 13,752 CRM leads reveals 14 actionable growth opportunities backed by hard conversion data. The headline: **mothers convert 38% better than fathers end-to-end**, validating a mothers-first Meta campaign. But the deeper findings — cultural sub-segments within NRI, the "dead leads that still paid" resurrection effect, assessment type performance gaps, trial velocity patterns, and timing optimizations — represent a larger combined revenue opportunity.

**Key corrections applied during analysis:**
- Cohort maturity bias corrected in monthly conversion trends (Section 8)
- WhatsApp column mislabeled in CRM — contains campaign names, not WhatsApp engagement data. Removed from findings.
- All copy recommendations validated against MathFit brand guidelines
- 3,922 blank rows (zero data in all fields) excluded from all calculations

## SECTION 2: THE TRIAL VELOCITY PARADOX

### Finding: Parents who take longer to trial convert better

| Days from Lead to Trial | TD → Paid Rate |
|------------------------|----------------|
| 0-7 days | 29.0% |
| 8-14 days | 31.2% |
| 15-30 days | 33.1% |
| **30+ days** | **34.7%** |

**Median time to trial:** 5 days (overall), but the highest-converting cohort takes 30+ days.

**What this means:** Deliberate parents — those who research, compare, and decide on their own timeline — arrive at the trial with higher commitment. High-pressure urgency messaging ("Limited slots! Book now!") may be pulling in lower-intent leads at the expense of thoughtful decision-makers.

### Recommended Actions
- **Extend nurture sequences to 45+ days.** Stop treating 14-day non-bookers as dead leads.
- **Segment nurture content by stage:**
  - Day 1-7: Social proof (other parents' experiences)
  - Day 8-21: Philosophy ("We teach children to think, not just calculate")
  - Day 22-45: Deep transformation stories from parents who took their time deciding
- **Remove urgency-based CTAs** from all nurture emails — they conflict with brand tone ("calm, confident, intelligent") and the data says they don't help conversion

**Estimated impact:** Growing the 30+ day trial cohort by 20% = ~30 additional paid conversions per quarter at the highest conversion rate.

## SECTION 3: TRIAL NO-SHOW ECONOMICS

### Finding: 26.1% of scheduled trials never happen — 1,029 lost leads

| Dimension | Lowest No-Show | Highest No-Show |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Day of week | Monday (21.6%) | Sunday (29.4%) |
| Channel | Google Brand (17.5%) | Others (variable) |
| Ethnicity | NRI (21.9%) | Asian-Vietnamese (90.9%) |
| Market | APAC (24.6%) | UK (35.8%) |

**The Monday Effect:** An 8-percentage-point gap between Monday and Sunday no-show rates. If all trials were scheduled Monday-Thursday, we'd recover ~80 additional completed trials per quarter.

### Recommended Actions
- **Default trial scheduling to Monday-Thursday** — deprioritize or eliminate weekend slots
- **UK-specific pre-trial engagement:** 35.8% no-show rate suggests timezone friction or different onboarding expectations. Consider a UK-tailored confirmation sequence.
- **Pre-trial reminder sequence:** SMS/WhatsApp reminder 24 hours and 2 hours before trial
- **No-show follow-up:** Immediate rescheduling offer within 1 hour of missed trial

**Estimated impact:** Reducing no-shows from 26% to 20% = ~240 additional trials done per year → ~80 additional paid at 33% TD→Paid.

**Estimated impact:** Doubling the return rate through proactive retargeting and sibling campaigns = +76 paid per year.

## SECTION 6: WARM LEAD RECOVERY

### Finding: "Not now" leads convert at 29% — 4x the baseline

**233 leads** dispositioned as "Will join later" / "Need time to decide" / "Waiting for next term" eventually convert at **29.2%** (68 paid).

**Verified:** Paid dates are 1-6 months after the "not now" disposition — these parents genuinely returned and enrolled. Lead created Aug 2024, paid Feb 2026. Lead created Oct 2025, paid Jan 2026.

**Profile:** 56% US, 32% ME, mostly NRI, concentrated in Grades 2-5.

**This is the lowest-hanging fruit in the entire funnel.** These parents have already qualified, trialed, and expressed intent. They just weren't ready yet.

### Recommended Actions
- **Automated re-engagement:** Trigger at 30/60/90 days after disposition
- **Content:** Term-aligned outreach — "The new term is starting. Your child's learning plan is still ready."
- **Offer:** Preferred scheduling with continuity from their previous trial experience
- **Tag in CRM:** These leads should be flagged separately from "Not Interested" — they are deferred intent, not rejection

**Estimated impact:** Proactive re-engagement could push the 29% organic conversion to 40%+ → +25 incremental paid.

**Estimated impact:** Reducing pricing objections by 20% through better pre-qualification = ~137 leads saved → ~14 additional paid (at their lower base conversion rate).

## SECTION 8: MONTHLY CONVERSION TREND (Cohort-Corrected)

### Finding: Conversion has declined as volume scaled — but the magnitude is smaller than it appears

**Raw numbers are misleading.** Older cohorts (Oct-Nov 2025) had 4-6 months for leads to mature into paid customers. Recent cohorts (Feb-Mar 2026) have had weeks. Comparing them directly overstates the decline.

**Corrected comparison (30-day conversion window, apples-to-apples):**

| Month | QLs | Paid (within 30 days) | 30-Day Conv Rate | Maturity Window |
|-------|-----|----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Dec 2025 | 180 | 43 | 23.9% | 96 days (mature) |
| Jan 2026 | 3,027 | 293 | 9.7% | 65 days (mature) |
| Feb 2026 | 3,599 | 267 | 7.4% | 37 days (mostly mature) |
| Mar 2026 | 2,431 | 173 | 7.1% | 6 days (immature — many March leads haven't had their full 30-day window yet) |

**What's real vs. artifact:**
- **Dec → Jan drop (23.9% → 9.7%):** Mostly a volume scaling effect. Dec had 180 QLs; Jan had 3,027 (17x). Small-sample months always look better. Jan at scale is the true baseline.
- **Jan → Feb decline (9.7% → 7.4%):** This is real. Both months are fully matured. A 24% relative decline in conversion at similar volume.
- **Feb → Mar (7.4% → 7.1%):** Cannot be confirmed yet. March only has 6 days of maturity past month-end — late-March leads haven't had time to convert. Revisit in mid-April.

### Recommended Actions
- **Investigate the Jan → Feb conversion drop.** Possible causes:
  - Did audience targeting broaden beyond core NRI segments in February?
  - Did sales team response time degrade as volume sustained above 3,000 QLs/month?
  - Was there creative fatigue (same ads running for 30+ days)?
- **Check Feb cohort ethnicity mix** — if the NRI share dropped, that alone explains the conversion decline
- **Monitor March cohort weekly** until it reaches 30-day maturity (mid-April)

**Estimated impact:** If the Jan→Feb decline is addressable (audience drift or sales capacity), restoring Feb to Jan conversion levels = +56 additional paid from Feb alone.

## SECTION 11: ASSESSMENT TYPE — THE HIDDEN 2X MULTIPLIER

### Finding: MCNA assessment converts at 2x the rate of ECNA

| Assessment Type | QLs | QL→TD | TD→Paid | QL→Paid |

- **If MCNA drives commitment:** Expand MCNA as the default assessment for all leads. A shift from 12.9% to 22.1% conversion on the existing base would be transformative.
- **Fix the blank assessment gap:** 7,927 leads with no assessment type = broken onboarding flow. Ensure every QL gets routed to an assessment.

## SECTION 14: THE FUNNEL WATERFALL — WHERE THE MONEY LEAKS

### Full Funnel (9,830 QLs)

```
QLs:              9,830  (100%)
                    ↓ 40.1% schedule a trial
Trial Scheduled:  3,941  
                    ↓ 73.9% show up (26.1% no-show)
Trial Done:       2,912  
                    ↓ 33.1% convert to paid
Paid:               963  (9.8% of QLs)
```

### The Two Critical Leaks

**Leak 1: QL → Trial Scheduled (59.9% drop — 5,889 leads never schedule)**
This is the largest absolute leak. Nearly 6,000 qualified leads never even book a trial. Possible causes: slow sales follow-up, unclear next steps, or leads not being ready (see Section 2 on the speed paradox — some of these may convert later).

**Leak 2: Trial Done → Paid (66.9% drop — 1,949 "almost converted")**
2,046 leads completed a trial but didn't pay. Their profile is nearly identical to paid customers — same ethnicity, same grades, same channels. The #1 difference: **53% have no follow-up disposition** (process gap) and **15.3% cite pricing** (5x the rate of paid leads).

### NRI vs Non-NRI Funnel Comparison

| Stage | NRI | Non-NRI |
|-------|-----|---------|
| QLs | 6,126 (100%) | 3,704 (100%) |
| Trial Done | 2,445 (39.9%) | 467 (12.6%) |
| Paid | 819 (13.4%) | 144 (3.9%) |

The non-NRI funnel breaks at the **QL → Trial** stage (only 12.6% trial vs NRI's 39.9%). Once non-NRI leads actually trial, their close rate (30.8%) is not dramatically worse than NRI's (33.5%). **The problem is getting non-NRI leads to trial, not closing them once they're there.**

### The "Almost Converted" Segment (2,046 leads)

These trialed-but-didn't-pay leads are the highest-ROI recovery opportunity in the funnel:
- **53% have no disposition** — no one followed up, or follow-up wasn't logged. Pure process failure.
- **15.3% cite pricing** — they liked it but couldn't justify the cost. 5x the pricing objection rate of paid leads.
- **4.3% joined a competitor** — they evaluated and chose someone else.

### Recommended Actions
- **Audit the 53% with no disposition:** Are these sales process gaps (no follow-up) or CRM logging gaps? Either way, fixing this is the single highest-volume opportunity.
- **Post-trial pricing conversation redesign:** 15.3% pricing objection rate suggests the value isn't being communicated during or immediately after the trial. The trial itself should demonstrate the MathFit ecosystem, not just teach a lesson.
- **7-day post-trial nurture sequence** for all trial-done leads who don't convert immediately — parent testimonials, tutor follow-up, "here's what we'd work on next" personalization.

## DATA QUALITY FLAGS

These issues limit the depth of analysis and should be addressed to unlock the next level of insight:

1. **3,922 blank rows (28.5% of dataset):** Zero data in all fields — no name, country, ethnicity, channel, or conversion status. These are not leads; they appear to be empty/corrupted records. Should be cleaned from the CRM export.
2. **net_booking values are all < 0.04:** Revenue per paid customer shows values like 0.019, 0.001 — clearly not real currency amounts. Revenue tracking appears broken, which blocks any ROAS, LTV, or revenue-per-segment analysis.
3. **"whatsapp category" column is mislabeled:** Contains campaign names (Google SEM, Facebook LAL, etc.), not WhatsApp engagement data. Only 17 leads actually came through WhatsApp campaigns.
4. **Campaign naming not standardized:** 93% of campaigns categorize as "Unknown" targeting type. Cannot optimize audience strategy without knowing which audiences are being targeted.
5. **email_id deduplication likely broken:** Sibling/multi-child analysis returned only 2 families across 9,830 leads — statistically impossible. The email_id field may not be properly populated.

## METHODOLOGY NOTES

- **Gender classification:** First names matched against a 1,500+ name dictionary covering South Asian, Middle Eastern, and Western names. 66% of named leads remained unclassified (ambiguous names). All gender findings are directional, based on the 34% that could be classified.
- **Cohort correction:** Monthly conversion rates use a 30-day conversion window to enable apples-to-apples comparison across months with different maturity periods.
- **Minimum sample thresholds:** Micro-segment analysis requires minimum 20 leads for inclusion.
- **Brand compliance:** All copy recommendations validated against MathFit brand guidelines — no time-bound promises, no urgency tactics, no transactional framing, no guarantee language.
