## MathFit™ Framework

MathFit™ is Cuemath's proprietary concept — **mathematical fitness**. Just like physical fitness, it has two dimensions:

### Skills Dimension (FUAR)

| Dimension | What It Means | In Copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Fluency** | Accurate, efficient execution of operations | "Build accuracy and fluency in core operations" |
| **Understanding** | Deep conceptual clarity — the "why" behind the "what" | "Understand *why* the formula works, not just *how* to use it" |
| **Application** | Solving word problems and real-world math | "Apply math to real-world problems" |
| **Reasoning** | Logical thinking, pattern recognition, strategy | "Reason through problems, not just memorise steps" |

### Mindset Dimension

| Dimension | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Interest** | Genuine curiosity about math |
| **Confidence** | Belief in one's ability to tackle unfamiliar problems |
| **Growth Mindset & Resilience** | Willingness to struggle productively |
| **Math Relevance** | Seeing math as useful beyond school |

### MathFit Is / Is Not

| MathFit IS | MathFit IS NOT |
|---|---|
| Thinking | Speed tricks |
| Understanding | Finishing worksheets |
| Confidence | Memorising formulas |
| Reasoning through unfamiliar problems | Racing to answers |

**Rules:**
- Skills dimension order: Fluency, Understanding, Application, Reasoning (FUAR). Never reorder.
- Mindset dimension is equally important — don't reduce MathFit to just skills.
- ™ symbol on first mention in any piece of content. After first prominent mention, ™ can be omitted in body copy but must reappear in section headers, end frames, and sign-offs.
- Correct: MathFit™. Incorrect: MathFit / mathfit / Math Fit.
- MathFit is NOT about speed, tricks, or shortcuts. It's about thinking.
- The core frame: "AI calculates. Humans must think. MathFit is the thinking that matters."

> **Note for Forge:** The Cuemath App (MathGym) uses a 5-skill model that includes Memory. The MathFit Constitution (which governs brand and marketing) uses FUAR (4 skills). For **marketing copy**, always use FUAR. For **app-specific copy** (App Store descriptions, in-app messaging), Memory may be included as a practice dimension.

## The Cuemath Way — Core Pedagogy

### "Cue, Don't Tell"
Tutors don't give answers. They ask the right questions so the child discovers answers on their own. This builds thinking skills, not dependency.

### Productive Struggle
Problems are never too easy, never overwhelming — just hard enough to stretch thinking. Struggle is the path, not a bug.

### High Talk Ratio
The child does the thinking, talking, explaining. The tutor cues, nudges, challenges. The **Talk-o-Meter** measures how much the student talks during class — because when kids verbalise, explain, and teach back, they retain concepts longer and build stronger memory connections.

### Interleaved Learning
Mixes different types of problems instead of repeating the same kind. Helps children recognise patterns and apply concepts flexibly.

### Neuroscience-Backed
The Cuemath Way leverages: productive struggle, interleaving, retrieval practice, explanation, and teaching back.

## Positioning Rules (from Constitution)

### Always Do
- Sound calm, confident, intelligent
- Speak to parents as thinking adults
- Feel long-term and trustworthy
- Make MathFit feel obvious and aspirational
- Ladder every ad back to: "Cuemath will make my child MathFit for the AI world"

### Never Do
- Promise shortcuts
- Hype speed or tricks
- Say "guaranteed marks" or "guaranteed grades"
- Over-index on ease or fun
- Use childish exaggeration, loud colours, or gimmicky visuals

### The Core Frame
We don't say: "Math is easy."
We say: "Math makes sense when taught the right way."

### The AI-Era Frame
"AI calculates. Humans must think." — Use this contrast often:
- Calculating = mechanical, procedural, fragile
- Thinking = reasoning, flexible, durable

### The North Star
If someone sees **only one Cuemath ad** in their life, they should walk away thinking: "This company isn't just teaching math. They're preparing kids to think in the AI world."

## Language Rules

### Never Use
| Banned | Why |
|---|---|
| "classroom" | Cuemath is digital-first. No physical classrooms. |
| "center" / "centre" | Cuemath is online, not a learning center. |
| "unlock potential" | Generic edtech filler. Says nothing specific. |
| "bright future" | Generic edtech filler. |
| "love for learning" | Generic edtech filler. |
| "amazing" / "incredible" / "powerful" | Empty adjectives. Zero signal. |
| "capable, strong and confident" | Incorrect brand language (see below). |
| "kiddish" hooks | Off-tone for parent-facing brand. |
| "guaranteed marks" / "guaranteed grades" | Constitution: never promise guaranteed outcomes. |
| "quick results" / "fast improvement" | Constitution: never promise shortcuts. |
| "math is easy" | We say "math makes sense when taught the right way." |
| "speed tricks" / "shortcuts" | MathFit is about thinking, not calculating faster. |

### Always Use Instead
| Instead of | Use |
|---|---|
| "capable, strong and confident" | "think clearly, reason deeply, solve confidently" |
| "classroom learning" | "online learning" / "1:1 sessions" |
| "our centre" | "our program" / "Cuemath's platform" |
| Generic confidence | Specific outcome: "reason through problems, not just memorise steps" |
| "speed and accuracy" (for Fluency) | "accuracy and fluency" — MathFit is not about speed tricks |

### Copy Principles

1. **Outcomes over feelings.** What the child will be able to DO, not how they will FEEL.
   - ❌ "Your child will feel more confident"
   - ✅ "Your child will reason through problems, not just memorise steps"

2. **Parent-facing always.** Never address children directly in ad copy.
   - ❌ "Learn math the fun way!"
   - ✅ "Give your child a math foundation that lasts."

3. **Specific over abstract.** Name the observable outcome.
   - ❌ "Lasting confidence"
   - ✅ "Confidence you can see in their next test"

4. **No duplicate words** within a single caption, headline set, or body copy block. Includes morphological variants ("build" / "building").

5. **Anchor in the framework.** When possible, tie copy back to a specific MathFit dimension rather than speaking in generalities.

## Geography-Specific Language

### US Market
- "Tutoring" (not "tuition")
- Grade levels: Grade 2, Grade 3... Grade 12
- "Math" (not "Maths")
- References: SAT, AP, AMC, MATHCOUNTS, college readiness
- NRI audience: "Indian tutors" / "tutors from India" is a winning hook

### India Market
- "Tuition" (not "tutoring")
- Grade/Class used interchangeably
- "Maths" (not "Math")
- References: CBSE, ICSE, IB, Olympiad, board exams
- Currency: ₹ / INR. "4 Lakh+ parents" (not "400,000+")

### Australia Market
- "Tutoring"
- Year levels: Year 2, Year 3... Year 8 (LP stops at Year 8, not Year 12)
- "Maths"
- References: NAPLAN, selective school entry, Australian curriculum
- Seasons are reversed (summer = Dec–Feb)

### MEA Market
- Overlapping school calendars across countries
- Islamic calendar priority for seasonal content
- Multi-language consideration (Arabic, Hindi, English)
