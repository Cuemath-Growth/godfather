Copywriting formulas to use:


This handbook is designed for training. Each section starts with the Formula Definition, followed by a Mapped Script where every sentence is explicitly tagged to its corresponding part of the formula.
________________


1. PAS (Problem – Agitate – Solution)
Formula:
                                                * Problem: State a specific frustration.
                                                * Agitate: Explain the emotional or long-term cost of that problem.
                                                * Solution: Introduce the Cuemath way as the relief.
Mapped Script:
                                                * (Problem): "Math homework used to end in tears at our house every single night."
                                                * (Agitate): "Watching my daughter lose her confidence was the hardest part—she felt 'slow' because she was trying to memorize steps she didn't actually understand."
                                                * (Solution): "We switched to Cuemath, where tutors don't just give the answers—they help kids understand the logic behind the numbers."
                                                * (CTA): "Stop the homework battle. Book a free trial today."
________________


2. BAB (Before – After – Bridge)
Formula:
                                                * Before: The "Chaos" state (life before Cuemath).
                                                * After: The "Success" state (life after Cuemath).
                                                * Bridge: The specific part of the Cuemath method that caused the change.
Mapped Script:
                                                * (Before): "Two months ago, I had to sit over my son for two hours just to get him through one page of math."
                                                * (After): "Yesterday, I found him doing his homework on his own—with a smile on his face."
                                                * (Bridge): "The difference was switching to a method that focuses on why math works, rather than just how to get the answer."
                                                * (CTA): "Get your evenings back. Try a free session today."
________________


3. AIDA (Attention – Interest – Desire – Action)
Formula:
                                                * Attention: A bold, scroll-stopping hook.
                                                * Interest: A surprising fact or insight that keeps them watching.
                                                * Desire: The emotional payoff of the product.
                                                * Action: A clear command.
Mapped Script:
                                                * (Attention): "Stop telling your kids to memorize math formulas!"
                                                * (Interest): "Most kids struggle because they are taught to follow a recipe. But when the recipe changes on a test, they get lost."
                                                * (Desire): "Cuemath builds 'Math Logic.' Our students learn to explain their reasoning, so the concepts actually stick for life."
                                                * (Action): "Build a foundation that lasts. Book a free trial now."
________________


4. FAB (Feature – Advantage – Benefit)
Formula:
                                                * Feature: A factual part of the service.
                                                * Advantage: What that feature does.
                                                * Benefit: How that makes the parent’s or child's life better.
Mapped Script:
                                                * (Feature): "Cuemath provides 1-on-1 sessions with visual learning tools."
                                                * (Advantage): "This means your child gets a personal mentor's full attention and can actually see how the numbers work."
                                                * (Benefit): "The result is no more guessing on tests and a child who feels genuinely smart in the classroom."
                                                * (CTA): "Experience the difference. Book a free class."
________________


5. 4Ps (Promise – Picture – Proof – Push)
Formula:
                                                * Promise: A bold claim of a result.
                                                * Picture: Helping the parent visualize a better reality.
                                                * Proof: A demonstration or evidence of success.
                                                * Push: A low-friction call to action.
Mapped Script:
                                                * (Promise): "We promise to turn your child into a confident, independent math learner."
                                                * (Picture): "Imagine a world where you never have to argue about math homework ever again."
                                                * (Proof): "[Video: Student explains a concept to a tutor] This is the 'Aha!' moment our students experience every day."
                                                * (Push): "Don't wait for the next report card. Click below to try it risk-free."

6. Myth vs. Reality (Contrast)
Formula:
                                                * Myth: A common but incorrect belief.
                                                * Reality: The logical truth.
                                                * Transition: How Cuemath delivers that truth.
Mapped Script:
                                                * (Myth): "A lot of parents think some kids are just born 'bad at math.'"

Formula:

                                                * Solution: How Cuemath removes that obstacle.
Mapped Script:
